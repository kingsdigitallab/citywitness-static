    In 1290, the house of Thomas Mathews was near the Chapel of St John. ‘Thomas Mathews’ is just one of the names of ordinary inhabitants of Swansea which survive in medieval charters and other documents. It was to Thomas’s house that the body of William Cragh was brought after his hanging in 1290 – other than that, we know nothing about him. The object linked to this location is a medieval bronze button. Again, this tiny everyday item connects us to the anonymous individual who once wore it: an ‘unknown citizen’ of medieval Gower who stands for all those who lived out their lives here, and whose stories whisper to us across the centuries.

    About this place

    The medieval chapel of St John was located in an area of open fields on the northern outskirts of the walled town of Swansea. Now the site of St Matthew’s church, historical sources indicate that the church was founded by Knights Hospitallers in the 12th century and was associated with a manor they owned. An engraving by Paul Padley from the 1790s shows the earlier church was much smaller, consisting of a simple rectangular building, which would have had a nave and chancel. This engraving also shows that the church was largely surrounded by fields, with a few houses close to the road into Swansea. The present church was built in the 19th century to meet the needs of the growing population of Swansea which saw the fields around the church built over with workers housing.

    Connection with William Cragh

    On an autumn day in the year 1290, William Cragh was hanged on Gibbet Hill by William de Briouze, the Anglo-Norman lord of Gower. Cragh was hanged as a rebel and criminal, together with another Welshman, Trahaearn ap Hywel. In an act of particular cruelty, Lord William forced Cragh’s own friends and family to perform the hanging. Cragh was lifted onto the gibbet first, and then Trahaearn was pulled up by a rope slung over the crossbeam. But one medieval witness at the execution tells us that Trahaearn was ‘big and heavy’, and this caused the gibbet to break. Cragh and Trahaearn were hanged for a second time and seemed to be dead. But was that really the case?
