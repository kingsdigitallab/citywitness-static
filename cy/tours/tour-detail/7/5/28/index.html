Porth y Gogledd

  • Tour information

    The North Gate was one of the points where entry into medieval Swansea was controlled. Swords, daggers and cannon balls found in Swansea remind us of its violent past. Many people met a bloody end here in the Middle Ages. Medieval Welsh poetry calls Swansea a ‘town of broken towers’ where the women are ‘altogether widows’ as a result of Welsh raids.

    About this place

    Safai'r Porth Gogleddol ar y Stryd Fawr, yn amddiffyn y brif fynedfa i'r dref o'r gogledd. Nid yw'r porth yno mwyach, ond gellir gweld olion waliau'r dref yma o hyd gydag aliniad Lôn Morris a Stryd y Brenin. Pan fu archaeolegwyr yn gweithio yma, darganfuwyd rhannau o ffos y dref ganoloesol a ddangosodd ei fod yn 10 metr o led ac yn 3 metr o ddyfnder. Roedd y Porth Gogleddol yn rheoli'r rhai a oedd yn cyrraedd ac yn gadael y dref ac yn gwahanu'r rhai a oedd y tu mewn i'r dref gaerog oddi wrth y rhai a oedd y tu allan, yn y maestrefi y tu hwnt i'r wal, a adnabyddir fel y Dref Uwch mewn dogfennau hanesyddol.

    Connection with William Cragh

    The North Gate was in the town walls on Swansea’s High Street, and many of the witnesses to the William Cragh story passed through the Gate as they moved between key locations. Lord William’s steward, John of Baggeham, goes through the Gate repeatedly as he visits Thomas Mathews’ house to see Cragh’s body and reports back to his master in the Castle. Lady Mary’s maidservants, Jovanta or Sunehild, also pass through the North Gate on their way to take almond milk to the recovering William Cragh.

    Medieval Object

    • Medieval bronze sword, found in Fforestfach (courtesy of Swansea Museum)

    This impressive sword speaks of the ever-present reality of violence in medieval Swansea. The North Gate was a point where entry to the walled town was controlled, serving to protect the city against attack. John of Baggeham, steward of William de Briouze in 1290, tells us how he and a band of other men carry arms with them to ensure that the power of the Anglo-Norman lord is enforced and the town is kept under control.
