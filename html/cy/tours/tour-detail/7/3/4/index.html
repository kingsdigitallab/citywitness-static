Tŷ Thomas Mathews

  • Tour information

    The role of Mary de Briouze, Lady of Swansea Castle, in the hanging of the Welshman William Cragh, is strange and mysterious. Mary’s husband, Lord William de Briouze, ordered the execution of Cragh. But Mary begged for Cragh’s life to be spared. When her husband refused, she then prayed to St Thomas de Cantilupe, a popular saint, to intervene. As Cragh began to recover, she sent her maidservants – and possibly her own daughter – to nurse him back to health. Why did Lady Mary care so much about a condemned Welsh outlaw? Could there be a story of secret allegiances or even forbidden love hidden here…?

    About this place

    Dyluniwyd y Castell Newydd i gymryd lle'r Hen Gastell, a dechreuwyd ar y gwaith i'w adeiladu tua 1284-90 dan arglwyddiaeth William de Braose II. Ychwanegwyd yr arcedau unigryw, a welir yn uchel ar y waliau sy'n sefyll, gan John, Arglwydd Mowbray (1331-54). Mae Castell Newydd Abertawe yn adlewyrchu cyfnod eithaf heddychlon heb lawer o ymosodiadau na rhyfeloedd. Dyluniwyd y Castell Newydd â'r nod o greu man cyfforddus i fyw a gweithio, gydag ystafelloedd preifat â'r holl gyfleusterau a thŷ bach y tu mewn a gâi ei wagio i'r ffos. Hefyd roedd neuadd fawr ar gyfer adloniant a busnes ar y llawr cyntaf, gyda nifer o isloriau oddi tano. Erbyn hyn mae tystiolaeth hefyd bod tai ar gyfer preswylwyr cyfoethocach Abertawe yn ward allanol y castell. Cliriwyd yr ardal hon yn y 1960au a'r 1970au gan ddatgelu adfeilion y castell, ac yna crëwyd Sgwâr y Castell.

    Connection with William Cragh

    After he was hanged, the body of William Cragh was carried to the house of Thomas Mathews, a burgess (townsman) of Swansea. His body lay here, bloody and disfigured, with his eyes hanging from their sockets and the tongue swollen out of his mouth. Adam of Loughor, aged around 13 at the time, tells us he was too scared to look at the body. And yet, to everyone’s amazement, William Cragh began to come back to life. Many witnesses came to see this and Lady Mary de Briouze – wife of Lord William who had ordered the execution – sent a maidservant with nourishing almond milk to help Cragh recover. Was it a miracle?

    Medieval Object

    • Bronze button, found in Penmaen Burrows (courtesy of Swansea Museum)

    We don’t know the exact location of the house of Thomas Mathews in medieval Swansea – and we don’t know who once wore this tiny bronze button. The button could have slipped from the clothing of any reasonably well-to-do citizen of medieval Swansea: it’s something quite ordinary and quite small, but which suggests an intimate and direct connection with an unknown man or woman in the medieval past of Swansea and the Gower.
